Before you begin

A note on privacy and purpose

What this tool is: Examined is a philosophical reflection tool supporting self-knowledge and values clarification. It is not a mental health service, not a substitute for therapy or counseling, and not designed for clinical use.

Your data stays in your browser: Your essays, profile, coherence readings, and conversation archive are stored only in this browser. Philosophy Lab Corporation has no database and cannot see what you write.

When you use the Companion, messages are transmitted: Each message travels via an encrypted connection (HTTPS/TLS — the same standard used for online banking) to Anthropic’s Claude API. Anthropic processes your message and a summary of your profile to generate a response. The connection is encrypted in transit — it cannot be read by anyone in between. Anthropic does not train its models on API data.

Write only what you are comfortable transmitting to a third-party AI service. If you prefer not to transmit, use Local Reflection mode in the Companion tab.

If you are in crisis: If you are experiencing thoughts of harming yourself or others, please contact a mental health professional or call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline) rather than continuing with this app right now.

This tool is designed for adults. If you are under 18, please seek a parent or guardian’s guidance before using it.

You can review this in the About tab at any time.

Your philosophical profile

Two steps, fifteen minutes. First, twenty-two scenario questions that map your moral architecture. Then, six questions about your life and how you became who you are. Together they generate a personal philosophical profile that describes not just your values but your specific way of inhabiting them.

The Examined app holds that you are a moral agent — a being with values, history, and commitments that are genuinely yours. It holds equally that everyone you encounter is the same kind of being. Self-examination and the recognition of others as self-examining beings are not separate tasks. They are the same project, approached from the inside.

Your Profile
What to do next — a guided start ▾
1
Read your profile slowly
Notice what feels precisely right, and what feels slightly off or missing. Both are useful. The profile is a first approximation, not a final word. The parts that miss you are where your examination has the most territory to explore.
2
Bring something real to the Companion
The Companion is not a chatbot and not a therapist. It holds your specific model and helps you think from within it. Reflect mode is the best starting point: bring a real situation — a decision you are navigating, a tension you are living with, something that is pulling you in two directions. The Companion will engage with it using your actual framework, not a generic one.
3
Explore forward or backward
Explore mode takes you forward: a decision you haven’t made yet, a future you are trying to imagine, a possibility you want to think through before committing to it. Experiment mode takes you backward: a choice you have already made that you want to understand better, a counterfactual, a moment from your past that still shapes how you see things now. Use whichever matches what is live for you.
4
Archive what matters
After a Companion exchange that felt significant — where something clicked, or where you saw something you hadn’t seen before — save it to the archive. The archive is the beginning of a longitudinal record of your examination. Over time it becomes more valuable than any single session. The profile revision at 90 days uses archived exchanges as additional context.
5
Check in when something changes
The six-domain coherence check-in is not a daily obligation. Use it when you notice something shifting — when your energy is low, when a situation is consuming more than it should, when you feel less like yourself than usual, or when something has gone better than expected and you want to notice why. The trajectory over time is more informative than any single reading.
6
When you can’t examine — recover first
There are moments when asking deeper questions costs more than it gives. When you’re exhausted, overwhelmed, or feel like everything is too much, the Companion’s Recover mode helps you reduce load before returning to examination. Low coherence is not failure. It is information about what you need. Recovery Mode identifies the kind of burden you are carrying and offers specific next steps calibrated to that burden at whatever depth feels possible.
∞
The longer view
The profile becomes more precise as you use the Companion and archive significant exchanges. At 90 days, a revised profile uses everything you’ve archived as additional context — the model grows with you. The examined life is not a one-time event. It is a practice. This app is infrastructure for that practice — not a replacement for the thinking, only a space that makes the thinking more possible.

About Examined

Privacy, purpose & limits

What this tool is, what it does with your data, and what it cannot do. Read this before sharing sensitive material.

What this tool is

Examined is a philosophical reflection tool designed to support self-knowledge, values clarification, and examined life as a practice. It is not a mental health service, not a substitute for therapy or counseling, and not designed for clinical use. If you are experiencing a mental health difficulty, please speak with a qualified professional.

Your data

What stays in your browser: Your profile, essays, archive, and coherence readings are stored only in this browser. Philosophy Lab Corporation has no database and cannot access what you write.

What is transmitted when you use the Companion: Each message travels via an encrypted connection (HTTPS/TLS — the same standard used for online banking) to a secure proxy, then to Anthropic’s Claude API. The connection is encrypted in transit — it cannot be read by anyone in between. Only Anthropic sees the content, processes it to generate your response, and does not train its models on API data.

Your messages are subject to Anthropic’s privacy policy. If you prefer not to transmit anything, use Local Reflection mode in the Companion tab.

What to write and what not to write

Write only what you are comfortable transmitting to a third-party AI service. You do not need to use your full name or include identifying information. The assembly history questions invite genuine reflection — the more specific you are, the more precise the profile. You remain in control of what you share.

If you are in crisis

If you are experiencing thoughts of harming yourself or others, or feel unable to care for yourself, please call or text 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline, available 24/7 in the US) or contact a mental health professional immediately. This app is not equipped to provide crisis support.

For students

Your participation is voluntary. Your instructor cannot see your profile, essays, or Companion conversations unless you explicitly share them. This app is not graded and your data is not accessible to Saint Petersburg College.
